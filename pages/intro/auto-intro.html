Day to Day Activity

1. Morning Routine

a. Review and Prioritize Tasks:

  • Check Emails: I start my day by checking emails and any messages from team members, project managers, or stakeholders. This helps me catch up on any urgent updates, changes, or issues that may impact my automation work. If there are any critical updates or notifications, I prioritize them accordingly.
  • Daily Stand-up Meeting: I participate in a daily stand-up meeting where the team discusses progress, plans for the day, and any obstacles we might be facing. This is an opportunity to align with the team on what needs to be accomplished and to address any immediate concerns or blockers.

b. Review Test Results:

  • Analyze Overnight Test Results: I review the results of automated tests that ran overnight. This involves checking test reports and logs to identify any test failures or issues that need attention. By analyzing these results, I can pinpoint which tests failed and why, which helps in prioritizing the resolution of defects.
  • Document and Report Defects: Any new defects discovered during the automated test runs are documented and reported to the development team. This includes providing detailed information about the issue, such as steps to reproduce, error messages, and potential impact, to facilitate a quick resolution.

2. Test Development and Maintenance

a. Script Development:

  • Write New Automated Tests: Based on the latest features or changes in the application, I develop new automated test scripts. This involves creating scripts that simulate user interactions, validate functionalities, and ensure that new features work as expected.
  • Enhance Existing Scripts: I also work on enhancing existing test scripts by refactoring them for better performance or to incorporate new test scenarios. This may involve optimizing script logic, improving data handling, or updating scripts to reflect changes in application functionality.

b. Framework Maintenance:

  • Update the Test Framework: Regular updates to the automation framework are necessary to keep it compatible with new versions of testing tools and changes in the application. This involves making adjustments to the framework’s structure, configuration, or dependencies to ensure smooth operation.
  • Optimize Performance: I focus on optimizing the performance of the test automation framework and scripts. This includes reducing test execution time, minimizing false positives, and ensuring that the automation suite runs efficiently and reliably.

3. Test Execution

a. Execute Automated Tests:

  • Trigger Test Runs: I initiate automated test runs as part of the CI/CD pipeline or according to a scheduled test plan. This ensures that tests are executed regularly and integrated into the development workflow.
  • Monitor Test Execution: During test execution, I monitor the process to ensure that tests run smoothly. This includes checking for any interruptions, errors, or issues that may arise during the test execution.

b. Review and Document Results:

  • Analyze Test Logs and Reports: After tests are executed, I analyze the logs and reports to assess the results. This involves reviewing test output, identifying any failures or anomalies, and determining the root cause of issues.
  • Generate Test Reports: I create detailed test reports that summarize the outcomes of the test runs. These reports include information on passed and failed tests, defect details, and any observations that might be relevant for the development team.

4. Collaboration and Communication

a. Team Interactions:

  • Discuss Issues with Developers: I collaborate with developers to discuss any defects or issues identified during testing. This involves providing clear and actionable feedback, working together to reproduce the issues, and assisting in debugging or resolving problems.
  • Provide Feedback: I offer feedback on the application’s functionality, usability, and overall quality based on the results of automated tests. This helps ensure that the product meets the required standards and addresses any potential improvements.

b. Meetings and Updates:

  • Attend Project Meetings: I participate in project meetings, sprint planning, and review sessions to stay informed about project goals, timelines, and requirements. This helps me align my testing efforts with the overall project objectives.
  • Share Testing Updates: I provide updates on the status of automated tests, including progress, issues, and any planned actions. This keeps the team informed about testing activities and any potential impacts on the project timeline.

5. Continuous Improvement and Learning

a. Stay Updated:

  • Explore New Tools and Technologies: I research and experiment with new testing tools, technologies, and methodologies to enhance our automation capabilities. This may involve evaluating new automation frameworks, exploring AI-driven testing solutions, or adopting cloud-based testing platforms.
  • Review Industry Best Practices: I stay informed about industry best practices and trends in test automation. This helps me incorporate the latest techniques and standards into our testing processes to ensure we are using the most effective approaches.

b. Training and Development:

  • Attend Training and Workshops: I participate in relevant training sessions, webinars, and workshops to improve my skills and knowledge in test automation. This includes learning about new tools, methodologies, and technologies that can benefit our testing efforts.
  • Work Towards Certifications: I pursue certifications in relevant areas of test automation to validate my expertise and stay current with industry standards.

6. End-of-Day Tasks

a. Review Day’s Work:

  • Summarize Accomplishments: At the end of the day, I review what was accomplished and assess progress against daily goals. This helps me ensure that all critical tasks have been completed and that any outstanding issues are noted for follow-up.
  • Update Documentation: I update any relevant documentation, including test scripts, frameworks, and reports. This ensures that all changes are accurately recorded and that the documentation reflects the current state of the automation efforts.

b. Prepare for Tomorrow:

  • Plan Tasks for the Next Day: I prepare a list of tasks and priorities for the next day based on the day’s progress and any outstanding issues. This helps me start the next day with a clear plan and ensures continuity in my work.
  • Sync with Team: I ensure that any important information or updates are communicated to the team before the end of the day. This helps keep everyone informed and aligned with ongoing testing activities.
